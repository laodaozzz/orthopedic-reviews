Evidence-Based Comparative Analysis: Cephalomedullary Nails for Intertrochanteric Fractures

A comprehensive interactive summary synthesizing Level I & II evidence on PFNA, InterTAN, and Gamma3 systems

Executive Summary & Key Findings

This analysis synthesizes high-level evidence (Level I RCTs and Level II meta-analyses) comparing three predominant cephalomedullary nailing systems for intertrochanteric femoral fractures (ITF): the Proximal Femoral Nail Antirotation (PFNA), the TRIGEN™ InterTAN™ nail, and the Gamma3™ system. Implant selection must be precisely stratified based on fracture morphology (AO/OTA classification) and the integrity of the lateral femoral wall.

Critical Biomechanical Threshold

A lateral femoral wall thickness < 20.5 mm definitively indicates an incompetent wall. This mandates the use of an intramedullary device (over extramedullary screws) to prevent iatrogenic lateral wall blowout and catastrophic failure. Level II

Core Conclusions from Evidence

  • PFNA (Helical Blade): Utilizes compaction osteosynthesis, preserving osteoporotic bone. Offers the shortest operative time and lowest blood loss, but is vulnerable to medial "cut-through" with improper positioning. Level II
  • InterTAN (Integrated Dual Screws): Employs a worm-gear mechanism for active linear compression, eliminating the Z-effect. Demonstrates superior resistance to varus collapse and the lowest failure rate (OR 0.16 vs. PFNA). Ideal for highly unstable (31-A2.2, 31-A2.3) and reverse obliquity (31-A3) fractures. Level I Level II
  • Gamma3 (Single Lag Screw): Uses conventional sliding mechanics. Provides adequate fixation for stable fractures but has higher rates of peri-implant shaft fractures and rotational instability in complex patterns. Level I

Fracture Morphology: The AO/OTA Classification Paradigm

The AO/OTA classification is the definitive framework for treatment decision-making.

31-A1: Stable Fractures

Intact posteromedial cortex. Extramedullary DHS is a valid option. Single-screw CMNs (Gamma3, PFNA) also provide excellent outcomes with benefits of minimal invasive technique. Level I

31-A2: Unstable Fractures

Comminution of the posteromedial cortex. Intramedullary fixation is mandatory. The choice between single-screw (PFNA, Gamma3) and dual-screw (InterTAN) systems is critical, especially for subtypes A2.2/A2.3, due to rotational instability. Level I

31-A3: Reverse Obliquity/Transverse

Most unstable pattern; lateral wall integrity is completely compromised. Cephalomedullary nailing is the undisputed gold standard. The InterTAN's robust dual-screw fixation offers the best biomechanical support. Level II

The Lateral Femoral Wall: The 20.5mm Threshold

Finite element analysis and clinical studies have quantified the critical role of the lateral wall.

A wall thickness < 20.5 mm is incompetent. Using a DHS in this scenario creates stress risers leading to iatrogenic lateral wall fracture, transforming the fracture into an unstable 31-A3 equivalent. Von Mises stress at the hardware entry point can increase by up to 89.26% in models with a 10 mm wall thickness. Level II

Conversely, intramedullary devices bypass the lateral wall, transferring load directly down the medullary canal.

Biomechanical Design of CMN Systems

PFNA: Compaction Osteosynthesis

The helical blade compacts cancellous bone radially, increasing local bone density and purchase in osteoporotic bone. This minimizes bone removal. The primary risk is axial "cut-through" if TAD is not optimal. Level II

InterTAN: Integrated Dual-Screw Worm Gear

The integrated dual-screw design actively compresses the fracture site and physically prevents independent screw migration, eliminating the Z-effect and reverse Z-effect. The trapezoidal proximal nail profile offloads stress from the lateral wall. Provides unparalleled rotational control. Level II

Gamma3: Conventional U-Lag Screw

Uses dynamic sliding through a single lag screw. Effective for stable patterns but has inferior rotational control in unstable fractures. The U-blade variant shows no significant advantage over the standard lag screw. Level II

Predictive Radiographic Metrics: TAD & CalTAD

Traditional Tip-Apex Distance (TAD)

For conventional lag screws (Gamma3), a TAD > 25 mm is a strong independent predictor of cut-out (OR: 3.72). Level II

For PFNA helical blades, the "safe zone" is narrower (15-20 mm). Both very high TAD (>25-27mm) and very low TAD (<15mm) increase cut-out or cut-through risk. Level II

Calcar-Referenced Tip-Apex Distance (CalTAD)

CalTAD, referencing the inferior calcar, is a superior predictor for helical blades and dual-screw systems. A CalTAD > 25 mm correlates with a 3.43 higher odds ratio of cut-out. Optimal positioning for InterTAN and PFNA is center-inferior to minimize CalTAD and leverage dense calcar bone. Level II

This document synthesizes current evidence on how Large Language Models (LLMs)—including GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity—can bridge the deliberate practice gap created by ACGME duty hour restrictions and limited faculty resources.

Patient-Reported Outcomes (PRO) Curriculum Integration

A vital component of modern orthopedic reform is the systemic inclusion of Patient-Reported Outcomes (PRO), essential for assessing treatment efficacy in elective joint surgeries such as TKA and THA.15

Study Evidence — Controlled Pilot Trial

In a rigorously controlled study (experimental n=25 vs. control n=25), residents exposed to a structured PRO curriculum scored significantly higher on comprehensive knowledge assessments (70.56 ± 5.89 vs. 58.68 ± 4.72, p < 0.001). The experimental cohort also reported deeper impact on medical cognition (3.80 ± 0.41 vs. 3.48 ± 0.59, p = 0.03) and greater satisfaction.15

Global Health Equity and Resource-Limited Settings

The scalability of low-cost, off-the-shelf generative AI has profound implications for global orthopedic education. Internet-accessible LLMs require nothing more than a smartphone or basic computer, functioning as a "great equalizer" in medical didactics.13 By utilizing free or low-cost LLM interfaces, orthopedic residency programs in resource-limited settings (e.g., Vietnam, various African nations) can facilitate simulated registries, advanced case-based learning, and AI-driven formative feedback—activities previously requiring fellowship-trained faculty.12

Cognitive Stratification by Question Type

The 2022 OITE study categorized questions into three types:5

  • Type A (recall of facts, 31.6%): Gemini achieved 76.9% accuracy
  • Type B (diagnosis & analysis, 20.4%): Claude achieved 70.7% accuracy
  • Type C (management & treatment, 44.7%): Gemini scored 67.4%
The Knowledge Implementation Gap

When assessed using the Buckwalter taxonomy, ChatGPT demonstrated adequate basic recall (Taxonomy 1: 54%) and interpretation (Taxonomy 2: 51%), but performance dropped significantly on application and problem-solving scenarios (Taxonomy 3: 34%).7 This indicates a persistent gap in implementing knowledge within nuanced, multivariate clinical scenarios.

The Multimodal Gap: Visual Diagnostics

Orthopedics is an inherently visual specialty. In the 2022 OITE, 38.8% of questions contained images. LLM accuracy plummeted from 65.9% on text-only questions to 34.1% on image-dependent questions.5

Critical Limitation

While providing text descriptions of imaging findings inflated ChatGPT's accuracy from 69.1% to 77.8%,17 real-world clinical practice requires independent interpretation of raw visual data. Current low-cost LLMs cannot natively perform complex computer vision tasks on orthopedic radiographs without bespoke convolutional neural networks (CNNs).5

🎓 Revolutionizing Case-Based Learning with Socratic AI

Generative AI fundamentally democratizes CBL by functioning as an adaptive, on-demand Socratic tutor, available 24/7.1 Rather than passively reading a case report, the resident interacts with the AI to uncover the clinical picture piece by piece through progressive disclosure.

Architecture of Socratic Case Processing

The underlying architecture relies on five interconnected components:1

  1. Case Processing Pipeline: Narrative cases are ingested by the LLM and broken down into structured educational vectors, identifying key teaching opportunities and common diagnostic pitfalls.
  2. Knowledge Representation Framework: Clinical data points are mapped into a semantic network. Elements are classified by diagnostic significance (e.g., open fracture = "high specificity"; generalized effusion = "low specificity").
  3. Phase Management System: The encounter is structured across five sequential clinical reasoning phases—History → Physical Examination → Testing → Management → Discussion.
  4. Socratic AI Agent: Instead of providing answers, the system uses the Socratic method. If a resident suggests immediate MRI for a routine meniscus tear, the AI prompts: "What specific physical examination maneuvers should be performed first to support this hypothesis?"
  5. Adaptive Guidance Levels: The LLM dynamically adjusts feedback based on perceived skill level—direct corrective guidance for PGY-1 interns; subtle nudges for PGY-4 residents.

RAG-Enhanced Architectures (MedTutor)

Systems like MedTutor utilize Retrieval-Augmented Generation to query localized knowledge bases, medical textbooks, and academic literature via PubMed and Semantic Scholar APIs.23 Retrieved evidence is filtered using state-of-the-art reranking models, after which the LLM generates educational content and MCQs directly related to the case. Expert evaluations found outputs of exceptional clinical and educational value, free from the hallucinations that plague standard LLMs.23

🔑 Key Pedagogical Advantage

When a resident utilizing an LLM for self-directed study encounters a hallucinated surgical approach or anatomical anomaly without verification, negative educational reinforcement occurs.4 RAG architectures mitigate this by grounding outputs in verified medical literature.

✍️ Prompt Engineering as a Core Residency Competency

The efficacy of an off-the-shelf LLM is heavily dependent on the precision, structure, and context of the input prompt. Minor semantic changes in prompt wording can dramatically alter the depth, accuracy, and educational utility of the output.6

Template 1: Step-by-Step Mastery (Zero-Shot)

You are an expert orthopedic attending surgeon. Break down the surgical approach for a Total Hip Arthroplasty (THA) via the direct anterior approach into a structured learning plan for a PGY-1 resident. For each step, include: 1. Anatomical landmarks and internervous planes. 2. A practical example of a common surgical pitfall. 3. A brief quiz question to test understanding. Format your response in numbered steps.

Template 2: Chain-of-Thought (CoT) for MCQ Generation

Studies utilizing models like OpenBioLLM-70B have demonstrated that Chain-of-Thought prompting yields the highest clinical alignment (quality scores >90.4) for generating board-style MCQs.26

You are a medical education specialist. Before generating the final question, first outline your step-by-step clinical reasoning for the following orthopedic topic: [TOPIC]. Then, based on that reasoning, create one ABOS-style multiple-choice question with 5 options (A–E), specifying the correct answer and a 2-sentence explanation for each distractor.

Template 3: Problem → Solution → Practice

Teach me about periprosthetic joint infection (PJI) following Total Knee Arthroplasty using a problem-based approach. For each key concept, provide: 1. The clinical presentation and diagnostic criteria. 2. The evidence-based surgical solution (e.g., DAIR vs. two-stage revision). 3. A brief practice scenario requiring a clinical decision from me.

Template 4: Contextual Prompting for RAG Emulation

Using ONLY the provided text pasted below regarding the European Bone and Joint Infection Society (EBJIS) guidelines for Septic Arthritis, generate a 3-part interactive case study detailing patient presentation, synovial fluid aspiration results, and the required surgical intervention.
Comparative Model Note

When managing septic arthritis, while ChatGPT and Claude provided greater content depth, Perplexity AI scored significantly higher in providing valid, verifiable reference support.27 Educators training residents in evidence-based medicine should prioritize internet-grounded models for literature synthesis.

🤖 Virtual Standardized Patients (VSPs) in Joint Surgery Training

Standardized patients (human actors) are invaluable for teaching history-taking and communication, but maintaining robust SP programs is logistically complex and expensive.28 Generative AI provides a scalable, low-cost alternative through VSPs.

Platforms such as SimConverse, PCS Spark, and the AI Patient Actor app allow trainees to interview digital avatars using natural language.28 Unlike static branching scenarios, the resident must actively navigate the conversation, asking targeted questions to uncover mechanism of injury, pain characteristics, and pertinent medical history.31

🔧 Cognitive Rehearsal and Surgical Planning Training

Geometric Limitations of Raw LLMs

A rigorous study testing ChatGPT 3.0's ability to mathematically plan a Medial Opening Wedge High Tibial Osteotomy (MOWHTO) exposed severe limitations.35 When provided with MPTA and JLCA metrics, the AI's recommendations yielded a Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of 2.96—indicating very poor model fit and high clinical inaccuracy compared to fellowship-trained surgeons.

Clinical Warning

Standard language models operate by predicting the next probable token in a sequence rather than performing mathematical computations. They lack the spatial logic and geometric optimization algorithms necessary for complex osteotomy planning.35 Relying on generic LLMs for calculating precise surgical angles or component sizing is strongly discouraged.

Free Visual Templating + AI Workflow

Programs lacking expensive commercial software can utilize free templating platforms like Detroit Bone Setter: acetabular component within one size of actual implant 43.1% of the time; femoral component 72.3%.11 Generative AI then streamlines the cognitive and administrative workflow.

SurgeryLLM addresses standard LLM limitations by incorporating context-specific guidelines via RAG. In simulated scenarios, it demonstrated superior accuracy in identifying abnormal pre-operative labs, flagging missing investigations, and aligning recommendations with established guidelines.37

AI-Augmented VR Simulators

For institutions with simulation budgets, platforms by VirtaMed, PrecisionOS, and OSSimTech provide hyper-realistic digital twins and haptic feedback.38 Machine learning algorithms assess learner movements in real-time, providing quantitative performance metrics on precision, speed, and tissue handling—enabling objective judgments of technical skills.13

⚖️ Ethical Constraints, Governance & Future Outlook

Algorithmic Bias and Artificial Hallucinations

"Artificial hallucinations"—the generation of confident yet entirely fabricated medical information—pose the foremost risk.7 Models like ChatGPT and Claude have a documented propensity to fabricate academic citations.7 Because models are trained on uncurated internet datasets rather than exclusively on peer-reviewed literature, they are susceptible to algorithmic bias and may reflect historical health disparities.4

The Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Imperative

AI deployment in residency training must enforce a strict Human-in-the-Loop paradigm.8 Faculty must review AI-generated case studies for accuracy and debrief residents after VSP interactions to ensure the AI did not reward flawed clinical reasoning.8

Data Privacy

Inputting real patient data into public commercial LLMs risks violating HIPAA or regional data protection laws.20 Programs must enforce policies requiring complete de-identification of all clinical data before prompt engineering, or invest in secure enterprise-level API access.5

Cognitive Over-Reliance

If AI instantly provides accurate diagnoses, residents may bypass the arduous cognitive struggle necessary for long-term retention.4 Curriculum design must leverage AI to increase cognitive friction, not eliminate it. Socratic AI frameworks ensure the technology functions as a cognitively rigorous sparring partner.1

  • Implement AI literacy training for all residents and faculty
  • Enforce strict HIPAA-compliant data de-identification policies
  • Maintain Human-in-the-Loop oversight for all AI-generated educational content
  • Use multi-model/ensemble approaches to leverage distinct strengths
  • Prefer internet-grounded models (Perplexity) for evidence-based training
  • Pair LLMs with free visual templating tools for surgical planning
  • Design prompts to increase cognitive friction, not eliminate challenge
  • Regularly audit AI outputs for hallucinations and algorithmic bias

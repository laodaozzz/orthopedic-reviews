🏥 Introduction to the JBJS Educational Ecosystem

For over 125 years, The Journal of Bone & Joint Surgery (JBJS) has served as the definitive source of peer-reviewed scientific information for the global orthopedic community, establishing itself as the gold standard in the field. Descriptive — Source §Intro [1]

Its authority is validated by indexing in premier academic databases — MEDLINE/PubMed, Scopus, and the Web of Science — with a 2022 Impact Factor of 5.3. Descriptive — [1]

Beyond Publication: A Service-Oriented Platform

In the contemporary digital landscape, JBJS has evolved far beyond a traditional publisher into a comprehensive and integrated digital platform — a complete educational ecosystem supporting professional development at every career stage. This transformation from a content-centric publication to a service-oriented educational platform is a significant strategic development. Expert Opinion — [2, 3]

Key Strategic Insight: The modern JBJS website is not merely an archive of articles but a sophisticated toolkit comprising interactive learning systems, CME packages, multimedia libraries, and programs that actively support the educational process itself, such as the Robert Bucholz Resident Journal Club Support Program. Expert Opinion — [2, 14]

The JBJS portfolio is a collection of distinct yet interconnected resources, each tailored to specific learning objectives. This includes a suite of six specialized journals, extensive CME activities, concise clinical summaries, and a rich variety of multimedia content. Descriptive — [3, 5]

📚 The Core Publications: Foundation of JBJS Knowledge

The bedrock of the JBJS educational platform is its portfolio of six specialized journals. This collection is not redundant; it represents a sophisticated content strategy where each publication serves a distinct and complementary purpose. This segmentation is designed to meet the varied information needs that arise throughout a surgeon's clinical and academic workflow. Expert Opinion — [4]

Workflow Example: A surgeon might read the flagship journal for a landmark clinical trial → consult JBJS Reviews for a broader understanding → turn to JBJS Essential Surgical Techniques for a procedural guide → and search JBJS Case Connector when faced with an unexpected complication.

1. The Journal of Bone & Joint Surgery (JBJS) — Flagship

Published twice monthly, this is the primary source of foundational evidence for the entire ecosystem. Its core mission is to publish only the highest quality clinical and scientific research deemed clinically relevant and likely to influence essential thinking among orthopedic surgeons. This is the journal of record for landmark studies and practice-changing research. Descriptive — [1, 4]

2. JBJS Open Access

A continuously published, online-only journal that provides free, perpetual, and unrestricted access to readers worldwide. Its mission is to expand the capacity of JBJS by disseminating a steady flow of evidence-based scientific studies, educational articles, and reviews from all sources and countries. Indexed in PubMed Central and Scopus. Descriptive — [3]

3. JBJS Reviews

Designed for efficient knowledge synthesis, this online journal continuously publishes thorough and critical reviews of clinical and scientific research on topics of importance to orthopedic surgeons. All articles are authored by practicing surgeons and faculty members recognized as experts in their respective fields. Descriptive — [4]

4. JBJS Essential Surgical Techniques

A highly visual and practical journal that leverages digital media to provide detailed, step-by-step presentations of surgical procedures. Each article is extensively illustrated with high-quality images and frequently features video excerpts. A key feature is the clear highlighting of indications, contraindications, potential pitfalls, and challenges associated with each technique. Descriptive — [4, 10, 16]

5. JBJS Case Connector

A unique journal focused on rare or unusual case reports and complications. Its primary innovation lies in its function as a searchable database — compiling symptoms, conditions, and demographic details to empower surgeons to find similar cases, identify emerging trends, and distinguish between truly rare events and repeated instances of a larger, unrecognized problem. Descriptive — [4, 17, 18, 19]

🎓 Structured Learning: CME & MOC Resources

JBJS has developed a sophisticated suite of resources specifically designed to meet Continuing Medical Education (CME) and Maintenance of Certification (MOC) requirements in a structured, efficient, and highly effective manner. Expert Opinion — [6, 13]

Strategic Unbundling: A notable aspect of the JBJS model is the strategic unbundling of CME from content subscriptions. A surgeon can purchase a comprehensive CME package to fulfill all MOC requirements without needing a full subscription to the journal portfolio. As stated: "A subscription to the JBJS Journals is not required to complete CME activities. Access to the relevant articles is provided within the CME activity." Direct quote — [13]

The JBJS Clinical Classroom: An Adaptive Learning Engine

The centerpiece of JBJS structured learning is the JBJS Clinical Classroom. This is not a conventional question bank but a powerful online learning system that employs "a biological model of adaptive learning technology". The platform's algorithm observes an individual's learning patterns and performance to deliver individualized content that targets their knowledge, skill, and confidence deficits. This approach has been shown to potentially save users up to 25–50% of their study time compared to traditional platforms. Descriptive — [6]

  • Content scope: Over 1,021 questions and 515 learning objectives spanning all orthopedic subspecialties [6]
  • Quality assurance: All material developed by clinical experts, rigorously peer-reviewed, and grounded in best available evidence and clinical practice guidelines [22]
  • CME credit structure: 1 AMA PRA Category 1 Credit™ for every 6 questions answered correctly; 10 SAE credits for every 100 questions answered correctly [8]
  • Total credits available: Up to 750 CME & ABOS SAE credits [6]

Article-Based CME Opportunities

Beyond the Clinical Classroom, CME credits can be earned directly by engaging with specific content. A primary source is JBJS Summaries — concise synopses distilling the current "State of the Science" for common orthopedic conditions across nine subspecialties. They are designed for efficient learning and are a designated source for earning CME credits. Descriptive — [9]

🔍 Curated Content: Article Collections & Summaries

JBJS addresses information overload by acting as an expert filter and sense-maker. Resources like the "What's New" series or the "Evidence-Based Orthopaedics Collection" provide context and interpretation that can be as valuable as the original research itself. Expert Opinion — [10]

Flagship Article Collections

  • Current Concepts Reviews: 132 articles providing comprehensive and timely reviews of the latest literature on focused topics [10]
  • What's New By Subspecialty: 109 articles offering an annual review of the most important recent scientific advancements within each orthopedic subspecialty [10]
  • Evidence-Based Orthopaedics Collection: Published quarterly; highlights three high-quality studies with abstracts and expert commentary [10]
  • AOA Critical Issues in Education: 162 articles in partnership with the American Orthopaedic Association, focusing on major educational issues [10, 15, 25]
  • Topical Collections: Dynamic collections on emerging topics including Artificial Intelligence (56 articles), COVID-19 (133 articles), Pain Management, and Large Database Research [10]

Subspecialty-Specific Resources

  • Subspecialty Channels: Dedicated channels curating articles, videos, infographics, podcasts, and topic collections for each specialty area. Available for $179/year per channel or included in the Platinum Package [26]
  • JBJS Summaries: Concise "State of the Science" synopses organized across nine subspecialties: Foot & Ankle, Hand & Wrist, Hip, Knee, Pediatrics, Shoulder & Elbow, Spine, Sports Medicine, and Trauma [9]

🎬 Multimedia Learning: Video, Audio & Interactive Content

Recognizing that orthopedics is a craft as much as a science, JBJS has invested heavily in multimedia resources. By ensuring video content is "supported by published outcomes and rigorously peer reviewed," JBJS maintains its evidence-based authority while bridging the gap between "knowing" the evidence and "doing" the procedure. Direct quote — [12]

Video Resources

  • Orthopaedic Techniques / Ortho Procedures: Over 4,000 videos demonstrating the latest surgical techniques, from basic to advanced, supported by published outcomes and peer review [11, 12]
  • Video Abstracts: Short (2–3 minute) audio-visual synopses of select scientific studies providing quick overviews of key findings [11]
  • Author Insights: Informal videos where authors share personal reflections on their research and its potential impact [11]

👨‍🎓 Resources for Trainees and Educators

JBJS demonstrates a clear strategic focus on the next generation of surgeons. This heavy investment in free or low-cost resources represents a long-term strategy to cultivate lifelong users. A surgeon who has relied on JBJS resources throughout five years of residency is highly likely to view the platform as an essential tool upon entering practice. Expert Opinion — [30, 31]

For Residents and Fellows

  • JBJS Clinical Classroom: A primary study tool highly recommended for OITE and ABOS Part 1 board preparation, containing over 4,500 "probes" (questions) and more than 3,000 learning resources [7]
  • OITE Prep Modules: Curated question banks based on official OITE blueprints. Pricing: $19.99 for one year; $34.99 for a two-year bundle (90-day access products) [7]
  • Free Subscription for CORD Residents: Through a partnership with the AOA, JBJS provides free subscription packages to residents at CORD-member programs (individual value: $255/year). Includes digital access to 24 issues of JBJS annually, the online PDF archive dating to 1889, and JBJS Reviews. [30, 31]

For Educators and Residency Programs

  • Robert Bucholz Resident Journal Club Support Program: Provides funding to US-based residency programs to enhance journal club activities, fostering career-long skills in critically evaluating scientific literature. Funds can be used for refreshments, venue rental, or visiting professors. [2, 14]
  • Clinical Classroom for Residency Programs: Group subscriptions include free administrative tools allowing program directors and faculty to monitor resident performance, create customized exams, and utilize premade examinations for OITE and board preparation. [7]

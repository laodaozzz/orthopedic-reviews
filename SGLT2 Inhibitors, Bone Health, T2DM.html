1. Introduction & Clinical Context

The convergence of type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) and skeletal health constitutes a profound clinical challenge, frequently characterised by "diabetic osteopathy." Patients with T2DM paradoxically exhibit normal or even elevated areal bone mineral density (aBMD) while suffering a significantly heightened risk of fragility fractures.1

The underlying pathophysiology is multifaceted:

  • Low bone turnover state
  • Alterations in bone microarchitecture
  • Accumulation of advanced glycation end-products (AGEs) within collagen matrix
  • Microvascular complications impairing bone perfusion and osteocyte viability

Consequently, traditional DXA is partially ineffective for assessing true skeletal fragility in this population, and glucose-lowering medication selection must be carefully weighed against skeletal safety profiles.

SGLT2 Inhibitors — Expanding Indications Agents such as empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, canagliflozin, and luseogliflozin now serve as first-line or early add-on therapies for patients with heart failure, atherosclerotic CVD, or CKD.5 However, their mechanism of action directly influences mineral homeostasis, raising long-term skeletal safety questions.8

Early alarm was triggered primarily by the CANVAS trial, which showed a statistically significant increase in upper and lower limb fractures with canagliflozin, prompting temporary FDA warnings.10,13 However, subsequent large-scale trials — EMPA-REG OUTCOME, DECLARE-TIMI 58, and CREDENCE — failed to replicate these adverse skeletal signals.10

2. Underlying Biological Mechanisms: The SGLT2i–Bone Axis

SGLT2 receptors are not robustly expressed in osteoblast or osteoclast lineages.8 Bone alterations are therefore downstream consequences of altered renal tubular dynamics, systemic metabolic shifts, and hormonal feedback loops. V

2.1 The Phosphate → FGF-23 → PTH Cascade

SGLT2 cotransporters and sodium-phosphate cotransporters are co-localised in the proximal tubule. SGLT2 inhibition increases intraluminal sodium delivery, which paradoxically enhances phosphate reabsorption.16 V

  1. Induction of Hyperphosphatemia — In a 2018 crossover study by Blau et al., canagliflozin 300 mg/day produced a 16% placebo-subtracted increase in serum phosphorus, detectable within 2–4 hours.18 II
  2. FGF-23 Elevation — Osteocytes upregulate FGF-23 to force renal phosphate excretion. Intact plasma FGF-23 rose ~20% above placebo, peaking ~12 h after the phosphorus peak.17,18 II
  3. Suppression of 1,25-Dihydroxyvitamin D — Elevated FGF-23 inhibits renal 1-alpha-hydroxylase (CYP27B1), producing a concurrent ~10% decrease in active Vitamin D.9,17 V
  4. Secondary Hyperparathyroidism — Reduced active Vitamin D limits intestinal calcium absorption. Combined borderline hypocalcemia and hyperphosphatemia stimulate PTH secretion by up to 25% above baseline.17,18 Persistent PTH elevation drives osteoclast-mediated bone resorption.17 II

2.2 Insights from Genetic & Murine Models

The "Sweet Pee" (SP) mouse model (Slc5a2 nonsense mutation, total SGLT2 loss of function) provides vital longitudinal data. At 25 weeks, SP mice showed reduced femur length and significantly reduced cortical BMD despite normal serum Ca²⁺, PO₄, PTH, and FGF-23 — suggesting chronic SGLT2 dysfunction may contribute to long-term fragility through mechanisms independent of sustained hyperparathyroidism.21 V

2.3 Mechanical Unloading Due to Weight Loss

SGLT2i reliably induce weight loss through urinary glucose excretion and mild fluid loss.6 Per Wolff's Law, reduced mechanical loading on the hip, femur, and spine triggers compensatory bone resorption.12 Clinical studies have correlated the magnitude of SGLT2i-induced weight loss directly with proportional increases in CTX (bone resorption marker).12 V

2.4 RAAS Activation & Acid–Base Shifts

Osmotic diuresis triggers RAAS activation and vasopressin release, which can stimulate osteoclastogenesis.10 SGLT2i also promote mild ketogenesis and a marginally lower systemic pH, potentially causing the skeleton to slowly dissolve mineral to buffer carbonates/phosphates.24 V

2.5 Counter-Regulatory & Osteoprotective Mechanisms

Protective Forces at Play
  • AGE Suppression: SGLT2i reduce AGE accumulation in bone collagen, preserving biomechanical elasticity and toughness.1,10
  • Anti-inflammatory Effects: Suppression of IL-6 and TNF-α reduces RANKL-mediated osteoclastogenesis.1
  • Oxidative Stress Reduction: Lower systemic oxidative burden protects the skeletal microenvironment.
The net clinical effect represents a tug-of-war between detrimental hormonal/mechanical forces and these beneficial metabolic improvements.

3. Evidence from Meta-Analyses on Fracture Risk

3.1 Overall Fracture Risk in the General T2DM Population

A robust 2025 frequentist meta-analysis of 117 RCTs across nine antidiabetic drug classes found that SGLT2 inhibitors were the only class demonstrating a statistically significant reduction in absolute fracture risk vs. placebo: OR 0.85 (95% CI: 0.74–0.98).10 I

This was reinforced by a 2024 network meta-analysis (NMA) of 13 population-based cohort studies comprising >1.06 million patients, utilising SUCRA probability modelling.16 I

3.2 High-Risk Subgroup: Osteoporosis & Prior Fractures

Critical Vulnerability Identified (2025 Meta-Analysis) A specific subgroup — female patients with T2DM duration >8 years and documented prior fragility fractures or established osteoporosis — demonstrated a significantly heightened incidence of adverse skeletal events on SGLT2i vs. active comparators.27 I
Standard age- and sex-based stratifications showed no divergence from overall protective/neutral trends.27

3.3 Mendelian Randomisation Evidence

A 2023 MR study using UK Biobank data with genetic variants (SNPs) in the SLC5A2 gene as proxies for lifelong SGLT2 inhibition found no causal association with reduced BMD at any site, clinical osteoporosis, or any of 11 major fracture types (all p ≥ 0.387).29 III

Interpretation Genetic evidence refutes the hypothesis that SGLT2 receptor inhibition inherently causes skeletal fragility. Observed fractures are likely driven by downstream metabolic shifts or external mechanical confounders.

4. Real-World Data & Observational Cohort Studies

4.1 SGLT2i vs. DPP-4i (VA / Medicare Cohort)

A retrospective cohort of >76,000 propensity-score-matched episodes (median age 69.3 yr, diabetes duration 9.7 yr) tracked fractures over extended follow-up. Results:32 III

  • SGLT2i: 1,431 fractures (18.2/1,000 person-years)
  • DPP-4i: 1,564 fractures (19.8/1,000 person-years)
  • Fully adjusted HR: 0.93 (95% CI: 0.87–0.99)

Consonant findings were replicated in South Korea31 and Hong Kong claim databases.33

4.2 Skeletal Safety in Chronic Kidney Disease

A population-based cohort of >75,000 older adults with T2DM + CKD in Ontario, Canada, compared SGLT2i vs. DPP-4i for hospital encounters for fragility fractures:34,35 III

  • 180-day weighted HR: 0.95 (95% CI: 0.79–1.13)
  • 365-day weighted HR: 0.88 (95% CI: 0.88–1.00)
  • No significant interaction with baseline eGFR category (p = 0.53)36

4.3 Post-Fracture Course: Vertebral Augmentation (Chang et al. 2026)

Key 2026 Finding Among 3,514 propensity-matched adults with T2DM + osteoporosis and first vertebral compression fracture, SGLT2i users within 6 months prior had a 40% higher likelihood of requiring surgical vertebral augmentation (vertebroplasty/kyphoplasty):5,6 III
• SGLT2i users: 12.5% required surgery
• Non-users: 9.3%
• OR 1.40 (95% CI: 1.13–1.73; p = 0.002)
This does not prove SGLT2i caused the initial fracture but suggests altered post-fracture healing dynamics.

5. Impact on Bone Mineral Density & Microarchitecture

5.1 DXA (Areal BMD)

Meta-analyses of >1,300 patients undergoing serial DXA over 52–104 weeks show no clinically significant BMD changes at lumbar spine, femoral neck, total hip, or distal forearm.8 Overall weighted mean difference: 0.08 (95% CI: −0.09 to 0.26).38 I

Note: Canagliflozin 300 mg/day showed a small but significant −1.2% placebo-subtracted decrease in total hip BMD over 104 weeks, correlating tightly with magnitude of weight loss.23 II

5.2 HR-pQCT (High-Resolution Peripheral QCT)

A 48-week RCT of luseogliflozin vs. metformin in elderly Japanese T2DM patients (≥60 yr) utilised second-generation HR-pQCT with micro-finite element analysis at distal radius and tibia.11 II

  • No significant divergence in cortical or trabecular microarchitectural parameters
  • Total structural integrity and mechanical resistance to failure perfectly maintained

5.3 Trabecular Bone Score (TBS)

Current systematic reviews of SGLT2i effects on TBS are severely constrained by a lack of primary RCT endpoints using this modality — a critical blind spot in the literature.42 Smaller retrospective analyses align with HR-pQCT, showing no accelerated deterioration beyond expected disease-related decline.39

The Remodelling Uncoupling Paradox SGLT2i induce a significant PTH elevation and CTX increase (resorption ↑), while bone formation markers (P1NP, osteocalcin) remain unchanged and ALP declines.8 This "uncoupling" — resorption accelerating while formation stalls — theoretically depletes bone mass over years. However, AGE suppression and anti-inflammatory effects appear to clinically override this uncoupling in most patients.

6.1 The Critical Role of Vitamin D Status

In a targeted challenge study, canagliflozin caused a 31.3% drop in active 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D within 5 days in Vitamin D–deficient patients (≤20 ng/mL). Prior high-dose Vitamin D₃ supplementation completely prevented this plunge.46 II

Clinical Directive Ensure replete Vitamin D stores before initiating SGLT2i therapy to blunt the downstream hyperparathyroid response.

7. Key Confounding Factors

7.1 Orthostatic Hypotension & Falls

SGLT2i function as potent osmotic diuretics, massively exacerbating orthostatic hypotension — especially in patients with diabetic autonomic neuropathy.8,16 One analysis reported a fall incidence of 17.1/100 person-years among SGLT2i users, with an OR for falls of 1.80 (95% CI: 1.10–2.92).48 III

7.2 Polypharmacy & Compounding Risks

  • SGLT2i + loop diuretics: Exponentially amplified volume depletion and fall risk47
  • SGLT2i + GLP-1 RA: Compounded fall risk (OR: 2.89), likely driven by rapid weight loss including lean muscle mass (sarcopenia)48

7.3 Drug Interactions: Denosumab & Bisphosphonates

Denosumab initiation was associated with a 32% decreased risk of incident T2DM vs. bisphosphonates (HR: 0.68).50 For combined SGLT2i + denosumab, the synergy is theoretically potent — but denosumab delays of 90–180 days precipitate catastrophic rebound fractures (HR: 1.9).53 Given SGLT2i-induced BTM uncoupling, a denosumab discontinuation could produce a massive, unopposed osteoclast surge. III

Research Gap: Specific cohort data tracking the three-way interaction (SGLT2i + denosumab + discontinuation) remains absent from the source document.

8. Research Gaps & Future Study Design

Almost all existing meta-analyses rely on legacy CV/renal outcome trials where "fracture" was an unadjudicated adverse event, guaranteeing underreporting of asymptomatic morphometric vertebral fractures.10

Recommended Prospective Study Framework

  • Target Population: Postmenopausal females aged 65–80, T2DM >8 years, DXA T-score ≤ −1.52
  • Imaging: HR-pQCT (distal radius & tibia), TBS from DXA, lateral VFA for morphometric vertebral fractures11,42,44
  • Primary Endpoint: New morphometric vertebral fractures at 12 & 24 months
  • Secondary Endpoint: Time to vertebral augmentation (kyphoplasty/vertebroplasty)5
  • Biochemical Tracking: Intact PTH, FGF-23, 1,25-OH Vitamin D, Ca²⁺, PO₄, CTX, P1NP, bALP at baseline, 3, 6, 12, 24 months3,18
  • Confounders to Control: Falls (daily diaries + orthostatic BP), body composition (BIA/DXA-derived lean mass), concomitant GLP-1 RA, loop diuretics, insulin, antiresorptive agents47,48
  • Stratification: All major eGFR categories (>90 to <30 mL/min/1.73m²)36

9. Conclusion & Clinical Directives

The clinical consensus has matured beyond initial CANVAS-era apprehensions. Exhaustive meta-analyses and million-patient real-world cohorts consistently demonstrate that SGLT2 inhibitors do not increase overall fracture risk in the general T2DM population and frequently confer protective skeletal benefits vs. DPP-4i.10,16,32

Key Clinical Directives for High-Risk Patients
  • Perform individualised skeletal risk stratification before initiation
  • Actively mitigate orthostatic hypotension and fall risk, especially with loop diuretics or GLP-1 RA co-prescription
  • Correct baseline Vitamin D deficiency before starting SGLT2i46
  • Consider antiresorptive therapy (e.g., denosumab) for osteoporotic patients — with strict adherence to 6-month injection schedule53
  • Implement aggressive post-fracture monitoring in SGLT2i users with osteoporosis, given elevated vertebral augmentation rates5
  • Monitor for sarcopenia in patients on combination SGLT2i + GLP-1 RA therapy

Future prospective studies utilising HR-pQCT imaging and serial FGF-23 tracking are vital to finalising the definitive clinical framework for this intricate metabolic–skeletal cross-talk.
